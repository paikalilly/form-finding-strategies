| **Core Argument** | Architecture does not resolve contradictions. It holds them. It is the site of meaning. The position does not choose a side. It occupies the between. |

Form-finding, in this position, begins not with shape but with the question: what sequence of atmospheres might this space offer, and what range of human states might it hold without prescribing which one is correct? |

The Zumthor and Pallasmaa reference: "Some of the first architects I ever read. They stuck with me. I've maybe forgotten how to explain that to people and forgotten a lot of the details of the text that I have already processed into the position." The kaleidoscope metaphor: "I really love this — it captures atmosphere, it captures my process, it captures the eclectic way I put things together and meaning is assigned by both architect and user, it's interpretive." This position is more socially driven than politically driven — not because politics do not matter, but because empathy is more powerful than abstract justice.
