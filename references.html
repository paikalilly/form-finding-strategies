Prompt 01 Memory Interpretation Tool: ChatGPT — Section 2

Source files: ChatGPT memory / prior conversations

Based on all your memory of me, in general, from your understanding of my personality, character, and values; what is my position with regards to the following: - what do i believe about architectural form-finding strategies in today's world? - how do social, political, and environmental issues affect my thinking? Do NOT base your answers only on recent chats and my masters thesis topic. i would like you to think back further to my interests, my identity and my stance, beyond architecture; understand the root values i uphold, and then draw that back to an architectural approach or set of belief systems. you should look for reasons within my identity, about why i want to be an architect, my frustrations, my aspirations. Pretend you are a detective. you should look for patterns, and use deductive reasoning to analyse me.

Prompt 04 Position Hierarchy Table Tool: Claude — Section 2

Source files: Document A — consolidated research; Document B — voice note transcript response

I am giving you TWO documents only. Do not use web search. Do not use outside sources. Do not add new theories, architects, references, case studies, terminology, citations, or frameworks. Do not "improve" the argument by importing knowledge from elsewhere. Your job is ONLY to reorganise and diagnose the relationship between these two documents. DOCUMENTS: Document A: "Transcript response to AI consolidation" — This is my personal voice-note response. It is the source of truth. It contains my actual priorities, rejections, doubts, emotional emphasis, critiques, and authorial position. Document B: "Consolidated Architectural Position" — This is an AI-generated consolidation. It contains useful material, but also generic filler, overreach, misreadings, and structure that should NOT be inherited. Important: Document A is not a standalone essay. Document A is a weighting layer applied to Document B. Document B is not the authority. Document A overrides Document B. Your task is NOT to write the final position document yet. Your task is to create a POSITION HIERARCHY TABLE that will help me later graft the documents into one coherent architectural position document. Create a table with these columns: 1. Theme / concept 2. What Document B assumes or claims 3. What Document A says in response 4. Verdict: accept / reject / revise / downgrade / promote / park for later / discard 5. Importance to my true position: 5=thesis spine, 4=major support, 3=useful but needs reframing, 2=background/context only, 1=discard or barely relevant 6. Essay role: core thesis / support argument / context / future research / appendix / discard 7. Reframe in my own language 8. Notes for later grafting Rules: - Do not create final headings yet. - Do not merge the documents yet. - Do not follow Document B's existing structure. - Strong emotional response in Document A is evidence of importance. - Repetition in Document A is evidence of unresolved importance. - If Document A says something is "interesting" but not central, downgrade it. - If Document A rejects a framing but keeps part of the idea, mark it as "revise." - If Document B uses generic architectural language, identify it as generic. - Be conservative. Do not invent certainty where the documents show uncertainty. After the table, give me: 1. The top 5 core themes that should govern the final position document. 2. The themes that should be downgraded or removed. 3. The themes that are useful but need further research before being used confidently. 4. A short statement of what my actual architectural position seems to be, based ONLY on these two documents.

You are assisting with Section 3 ("Developed Argument and Case Studies") of an architecture essay. You will be provided with: 1. The assignment brief. 2. My completed Sections 1 and 2. 3. A position hierarchy document identifying the final themes of my architectural position. 4. A research report containing proposed architectural precedents. Important methodological note: The research report has already identified and justified the precedents in relation to my position. I then reviewed the precedents visually and assigned intuitive preference ratings based on my immediate architectural response to the projects. Because my position explicitly argues that beauty, atmosphere, sensory intelligence, embodied experience, and intuition are valid forms of architectural judgement, these ratings should be treated as a meaningful part of the selection methodology rather than arbitrary personal preference. The ratings are: Teshima Art Museum — 9/10, PC Caritas — 9/10, Ca'n Terra — 9/10, House & Restaurant — 8/10, Bruder Klaus Field Chapel — 7/10, Telet—n Children's Rehabilitation Center — 6/10, Water Cathedral — 6/10, Igualada Cemetery — 5/10. Your task is to write ONLY Section 3: Developed Argument and Case Studies. Requirements: - Target approximately 600 words. Do not exceed 700 words. - Do not rewrite, critique, or restructure Sections 1 and 2. - Do not write the conclusion or reflection section. - Do not introduce new precedents or new research. - Use only the information contained within the supplied documents. Argument structure: The section should not read as separate precedent summaries. Instead, construct a single coherent argument supported by case study evidence. The argument should demonstrate how the selected precedents collectively support: beauty as a primary architectural value; form follows feeling held in tension with form follows function; atmosphere as a sequence of spatial conditions rather than a prescribed emotional outcome; architecture as the productive holding of contradictions; radical empathy and care as architectural acts; kinesthetic and sensory intelligence as valid forms of spatial knowledge. Writing style: Academic but readable. Avoid generic architectural clich—s. Avoid excessive theoretical jargon. Prioritise clarity and specificity. Write in a voice that reflects a personal architectural position being defended rather than a neutral literature review. Focus on atmosphere, embodiment, materiality, process, contradiction, and care. After the 600-word argument, provide: 1. A list of the case studies actually used. 2. A short explanation (1–2 sentences each) of why they were selected. 3. Three recommended images per case study. 4. A brief note explaining what each image demonstrates within the argument. 5. Suggested figure captions suitable for academic use. Do not fabricate citations or references. Use only information contained in the supplied documents. OUTPUT A PLAIN TEXT RESPONSE.
